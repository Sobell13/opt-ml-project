## Week 3: Sharpness and Hessian Analysis

We measured two geometry metrics for the final Week 2 checkpoints: random-direction sharpness and the top Hessian eigenvalue. Random-direction sharpness was computed as the increase in test loss after perturbing the trained parameters with relative radius rho = 0.01. The top Hessian eigenvalue was estimated using power iteration on test batches.

The best Week 2 model was SGD with batch size 64 and learning rate 0.01. This model achieved the highest test accuracy and had relatively low Hessian curvature compared with the sharpest Adam batch-512 checkpoints.

The Hessian results show that Adam with batch size 512 has much larger curvature than the other settings. This suggests that large-batch Adam tends to converge to sharper regions of the loss landscape.

However, the random-direction sharpness results are noisier. Some low-sharpness checkpoints do not achieve the best accuracy, and some high-accuracy checkpoints are not the flattest according to random-direction sharpness.

Overall, the results partially support the flat-minima hypothesis. SGD batch size 64 generalizes best and avoids the very high-curvature regime seen in Adam batch size 512, but sharpness and Hessian curvature alone do not fully explain all accuracy differences.
