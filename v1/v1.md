Both optimizers train successfully: train loss decreases monotonically for both SGD and Adam. Adam ends with slightly lower train loss, `0.1506`, than SGD, `0.1656`, so Adam is fitting the training data a bit faster/better in this run .

However, the test loss is not monotonically improving. For both optimizers, test loss decreases at first, then starts increasing near the end. Adam reaches its best test loss around epoch 8, about `0.2248`, but ends worse at `0.2659` . SGD also reaches its best test loss around epoch 8, about `0.2503`, but ends worse at `0.2816` . This is a mild sign of **overfitting after roughly 7–8 epochs**.

The final test accuracy is close: Adam ends at `90.88%`, while SGD ends at `90.11%` . But this final number hides the fact that SGD had a better test accuracy at epoch 8, `91.46%`, and Adam had `91.88%` at epoch 8 . So for both methods, the **best checkpoint is better than the final checkpoint**.

A good Week 1 interpretation would be:

> In this first baseline run, both SGD and Adam successfully optimize the CNN on Fashion-MNIST. Adam reaches lower training loss and slightly higher final test accuracy than SGD, but both optimizers show a train/test gap after several epochs: training loss keeps decreasing while test loss increases after around epoch 8. This suggests that future experiments should track the best validation/test checkpoint, not only the final epoch.
